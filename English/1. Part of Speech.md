Here’s a **detailed explanation of all 8 parts of speech** with **clear definitions, multiple examples, and full sentences** so you can understand deeply:

---

# 🧠 1. Noun (Naming Word)

A **noun** names a person, place, thing, or idea.

### 👉 Types of Nouns:

* **Proper Noun** (specific name): *Ravi, India*
* **Common Noun**: *boy, city*
* **Concrete Noun**: *table, dog*
* **Abstract Noun**: *love, happiness*
* **Collective Noun**: *team, family*

### ✅ Examples:

* **Ravi** is going to school. *(Proper noun)*
* The **dog** is barking loudly. *(Common noun)*
* She showed great **courage**. *(Abstract noun)*

---

# 🔁 2. Pronoun (Replaces Noun)

A **pronoun** replaces a noun to avoid repetition.

### 👉 Types:

* Personal: *he, she, it, they*
* Possessive: *his, her, their*
* Reflexive: *myself, yourself*
* Demonstrative: *this, that*

### ✅ Examples:

* **Ravi** is my friend. → **He** is my friend
* This is **my** book
* She completed the work **herself**

---

# ⚡ 3. Verb (Action or State)

A **verb** shows action or a state of being.

### 👉 Types:

* Action verbs: *run, eat, write*
* Helping verbs: *is, have, will*
* Linking verbs: *is, seem*

### ✅ Examples:

* She **runs** every morning *(Action)*
* They **are** happy *(State)*
* He **is writing** a letter *(Helping verb)*

---

# 🎨 4. Adjective (Describes Noun)

An **adjective** describes or gives more information about a noun/pronoun.

### 👉 Types:

* Quality: *beautiful, tall*
* Quantity: *some, many*
* Number: *one, two*
* Possessive: *my, his*

### ✅ Examples:

* She is a **beautiful** girl
* I have **two** pens
* This is **my** house

---

# 🚀 5. Adverb (Describes Verb/Adjective)

An **adverb** modifies a verb, adjective, or another adverb.

### 👉 It tells:

* How → quickly
* When → yesterday
* Where → here
* Degree → very

### ✅ Examples:

* He runs **quickly** *(How)*
* She came **yesterday** *(When)*
* The food is **very** tasty *(Degree)*

---

# 📍 6. Preposition (Shows Relation)

A **preposition** shows the relationship between a noun/pronoun and another word.

### 👉 Common Prepositions:

in, on, at, under, between, behind, with

### ✅ Examples:

* The book is **on** the table
* She is sitting **under** the tree
* He went **to** the market

---

# 🔗 7. Conjunction (Joins Words/Sentences)

A **conjunction** connects words, phrases, or sentences.

### 👉 Types:

* Coordinating: *and, but, or*
* Subordinating: *because, although*

### ✅ Examples:

* I like tea **and** coffee
* She was tired, **but** she worked
* I stayed home **because** it was raining

---

# 😲 8. Interjection (Emotion Words)

An **interjection** expresses strong feelings or emotions.

### 👉 Examples:

wow!, oh!, hey!, alas!, hooray!

### ✅ Examples:

* **Wow!** That’s amazing
* **Oh!** I forgot my keys
* **Hooray!** We won the match

---

# 🧾 Full Example Sentence (All Parts Together)

👉 **Sentence:**
**Wow!** Ravi quickly ran to the small shop because he needed a book.

### Breakdown:

* **Wow!** → Interjection
* **Ravi** → Noun
* **quickly** → Adverb
* **ran** → Verb
* **to** → Preposition
* **the** → (Article – part of adjective group)
* **small** → Adjective
* **shop** → Noun
* **because** → Conjunction
* **he** → Pronoun
* **needed** → Verb
* **a book** → Noun
